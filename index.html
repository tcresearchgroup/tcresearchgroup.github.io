About Our Research Group

Tri-Chandra Research Group (TCRG) is a non-political, autonomous academic research group of students and faculty based at Tri-Chandra Multiple Campus, Ghantaghar, Kathmandu, Nepal. Established on May 29, 2025 (2082-02-15 BS), our group is dedicated to pushing the boundaries of scientific knowledge across various fields.

Vision

Foster a collaborative academic environment where students and faculty engage in meaningful research, innovation, and dissemination of knowledge.

Mission

Support scientific inquiry through workshops, research projects, publications, open-source development, and strategic partnerships.

Objectives

  • Promote student and faculty-led research across disciplines
  • Organize seminars, workshops, and training programs
  • Create open-source academic tools and content (e.g., TriScan)
  • Collaborate with national and international research institutions
  • Uphold academic integrity and ethical research standards
  • Operate as a non-political, autonomous body focused exclusively on academic and research objectives

Research Areas

Plasma Physics

Studying plasma properties and behavior for applications in energy, space science, and technology.

Our plasma physics research investigates both laboratory and astrophysical plasmas. We study plasma instabilities, waves, and develop applications for fusion energy and plasma-based technologies.

  • Plasma Containment Methods
  • Plasma-Material Interactions
  • Space and Astrophysical Plasmas

Atmospheric Physics

Investigating lightning and atmospheric phenomena across Nepal's diverse topography.

Our atmospheric physics team studies lightning activities and their correlation with environmental factors, particularly in Nepal's unique geographical setting.

  • Lightning Data Analysis
  • Air Pollution Impact Studies
  • Regional Climate Patterns

Quantum Physics

Exploring quantum phenomena and applications including quantum computing principles and theoretical models.

Our quantum physics research focuses on understanding quantum coherence, entanglement, and the development of novel quantum algorithms. We are particularly interested in quantum measurement theory and quantum information processing.

  • Quantum Entanglement Studies
  • Quantum Computing Applications
  • Quantum Field Theory

AI & Machine Learning

Developing artificial intelligence and machine learning solutions for scientific research and analysis.

Our AI Lab focuses on developing and applying machine learning techniques to solve complex scientific problems and analyze large datasets.

  • AI for Scientific Discovery
  • Data Science Applications
  • ML for Researchers Program

Environmental & Material Science

Investigating environmental challenges and novel materials with unique properties for sustainable solutions.

Our materials science research involves the environmental impact assessment and characterization of materials for sustainable applications.

  • Environmental Impact Studies
  • Sustainable Materials
  • Renewable Energy Solutions

Microbiology

Studying microorganisms and their applications in health, environment, and biotechnology.

Our microbiology group studies microorganisms found in various environments in Nepal and explores their potential applications.

  • Microbial Diversity
  • Biotechnology Applications
  • Environmental Microbiology

Open-Source Software & Tools

Developing accessible academic software tools to support research and education.

Our software development team creates open-source tools to support academic work and research activities.

  • TriScan Plagiarism Checker
  • Research Data Analysis Tools
  • Educational Software

Particle Physics

Studying fundamental particles and their interactions to understand the basic building blocks of matter.

Our particle physics research focuses on theoretical aspects and data analysis from international collaborations.

  • Theoretical Particle Physics
  • Particle Detector Studies
  • Standard Model Extensions

Membership

TCRG membership is open to any student enrolled at Tri-Chandra Multiple Campus. We offer several membership types:

  • General Member - Open to all enrolled students
  • Executive Member - Elected positions with leadership responsibilities
  • Department Head/Vice-Head - Leading specific research departments
  • Faculty Advisor - Faculty members providing guidance and mentorship

Members have the right to participate in TCRG programs, with the expectation to follow research ethics and pay applicable membership fees.
